# UI Design Examples

## Example 1: Login Screen

### Color Usage
- Header: Blue (#2563EB)
- Background: White (#FFFFFF)
- Login Button: Blue (#2563EB)
- Text: Black (#111827)

### Why These Colors?
Blue creates a sense of trust and security, 
making users feel comfortable when entering their login credentials. 
The white background keeps the interface clean and easy to read.

---

## Example 2: Shopping App

### Color Usage
- Background: White (#FFFFFF)
- Product Name: Black (#111827)
- Discount Badge: Red (#DC2626)
- Buy Now Button: Green (#16A34A)

### Why These Colors?
Red immediately attracts attention to discounts and offers, 
while the green "Buy Now" button encourages users to take action. 
The white background and black text improve readability.

---

## Best Practices
- Use a limited color palette (3–5 primary colors).
- Maintain consistent colors throughout the app.
- Ensure sufficient contrast between text and background.
- Test designs for accessibility, including color blindness.
- Use bright colors only for important actions and alerts.

## Conclusion
Applying color psychology correctly helps create interfaces that are visually appealing, 
easy to use, and emotionally engaging for users.
