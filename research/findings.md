# Research Findings

## Overview
This study explored how different colors affect user emotions, decision-making, 
and overall user experience in digital interfaces.

## Key Findings

### 1. Blue Builds Trust
- Users associate blue with trust, security, and professionalism.
- Commonly used in banking, healthcare, and technology applications.

### 2. Red Attracts Attention
- Red is highly noticeable and creates a sense of urgency.
- Best suited for notifications, alerts, and limited-time offers.

### 3. Green Represents Success
- Green is linked to nature, growth, health, and success.
- Frequently used for success messages and finance apps.

### 4. Yellow Highlights Important Information
- Yellow quickly catches the user's eye.
- Should be used sparingly to avoid visual fatigue.

### 5. Black and White Improve Readability
- High contrast between black text and a white background improves readability.
- Simple color schemes help users focus on content.

## UI/UX Design Recommendations

- Use **Blue** for trust-related features such as login screens and payment pages.
- Use **Green** for success messages and positive actions.
- Use **Red** only for warnings, errors, or urgent actions.
- Ensure sufficient color contrast for accessibility.
- Avoid using too many bright colors on one screen.

## Conclusion

Color psychology has a significant impact on user perception and interaction. 
Choosing appropriate colors improves usability, accessibility, and the overall user experience.
